\section{Reference-PDF Framing}

\begin{frame}{Reference-PDF Thesis}
  \begin{block}{Central claim}
    Model parameters are treated as generalized coordinates, the loss as potential energy,
    and optimizer state as momentum evolving on a metric parameter manifold.
  \end{block}
  \begin{itemize}
    \item A single update family contains flat-gradient methods, momentum, diagonal adaptive methods,
      natural-gradient-style updates, entropy descent, and the full Hamiltonian-geometric optimizer.
    \item Modern optimizers are interpreted by metric structure, kinetic term, or potential splitting.
    \item The empirical claim is deliberately bounded: the method is not uniformly superior, but is
      strongest when full-metric structure is available.
  \end{itemize}
\end{frame}

\begin{frame}{Hamiltonian Formulation}
  \small
  \begin{align*}
    H(\theta,p) &= \frac{1}{2}p_i g^{ij}(\theta)p_j + L(\theta),\\
    \dot{\theta}^{i} &= \frac{\partial H}{\partial p_i}
      = g^{ij}(\theta)p_j,\\
    \dot{p}_i &= -\frac{\partial H}{\partial \theta^i}
      = -\partial_i L
      - \frac{1}{2}p_j p_k\,\partial_i g^{jk}(\theta).
  \end{align*}
  \begin{itemize}
    \item The second term in $\dot p_i$ is the geometric force induced by a coordinate-dependent metric.
    \item Dissipation is added so the dynamics optimize rather than conserve energy.
    \item The same structure supports memory and spectral-entropy corrections.
  \end{itemize}
\end{frame}

\begin{frame}{Generalized Update Rule}
  \small
  \begin{align*}
    p_{t+1} &= \beta p_t - \eta\left[\nabla_\theta L
      + F_{\mathrm{geo}} + F_{\mathrm{mem}}
      + \alpha\nabla_\theta S(g)\right],\\
    \theta_{t+1} &= \theta_t + \eta g^{-1}(\theta_t)p_{t+1}.
  \end{align*}
  \begin{itemize}
    \item $F_{\mathrm{geo}}$ captures metric variation.
    \item $F_{\mathrm{mem}}$ lifts exponential gradient memory into a rank-2 metric construction.
    \item $\nabla_\theta S(g)$ encourages a controlled spectral distribution for the metric.
  \end{itemize}
\end{frame}

\begin{frame}{Standard Optimizers as Special Cases}
  \scriptsize
  \begin{tabular}{@{}p{0.25\linewidth}p{0.37\linewidth}p{0.30\linewidth}@{}}
    \toprule
    Optimizer family & Hamiltonian specialization & Removed or approximated \\
    \midrule
    Gradient descent & Flat metric, overdamped limit & Momentum relaxes instantly; $F_{\mathrm{geo}}=0$ \\
    Heavy-ball momentum & Flat metric with finite momentum & No curvature, memory, or spectral force \\
    Nesterov-style momentum & Flat damped flow with lookahead gradient & Lookahead discretization \\
    RMSProp / Adam & Diagonal adaptive metric approximation & Ignores $F_{\mathrm{geo}}$ from metric variation \\
    Natural gradient & Full information metric & Usually no Hamiltonian momentum or memory force \\
    Entropy descent & Full/preconditioned metric step & Keeps $g^{-1}$ but drops momentum and memory \\
    Hamiltonian-geometric & Full metric with optional forces & Retains momentum, metric, memory, entropy, curvature \\
    \bottomrule
  \end{tabular}
\end{frame}

\begin{frame}{Architecture Ablation Reading}
  \small
  \begin{itemize}
    \item The report separates two metric choices: an unrelated toy metric and a true Hessian metric.
    \item Under the toy metric, correction terms have little reliable average effect.
    \item Under the Hessian metric, the spectral term is statistically significant, and stability depends on the full architecture.

  \end{itemize}

\section{Takeaway}

\begin{frame}{Limitations from the Reference PDF}
  \small
  \begin{itemize}
    \item Scale: all benchmark systems are small; the full $n\times n$ metric is mechanism evidence, not a production-scale replacement for Adam-family optimizers.
    \item Statistics: several results are single-seed point estimates; the strongest variance check is the modern-optimizer 10-seed sweep.
    \item Tuning: learning rates come from modest grids, not exhaustive optimization.
    \item Modern-optimizer comparison: the Hamiltonian-geometric run there is a diagonal reduction, not the full curvature-memory-entropy architecture.
  \end{itemize}
\end{frame}

\begin{frame}{Summary}
  \begin{itemize}
    \item The formulation is mathematically coherent once the spectral-force sign convention and memory-metric construction are explicit.
    \item The empirical evidence is strongest where exploitable full-metric geometry is present.
    \item Modern optimizer comparisons place the method in the leading group, but do not separate it decisively from Adam or Muon.
  \end{itemize}
\end{frame}
