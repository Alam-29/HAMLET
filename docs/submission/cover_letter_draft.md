Dear Editors-in-Chief,

Please consider our Article, “Hamiltonian–Geometric Optimization for Neural
Learning Systems: Exact- and Approximate-Metric Theory with Controlled
Ablations,” for the Learning Systems section of *Neural Networks*.

The paper contributes two conditioning theorems for Hamiltonian optimizer
dynamics, including an explicit stability bound for spectrally approximate
metrics, and tests the resulting framework through held-out equal-budget
tuning, paired multi-seed neural and physics-informed learning experiments,
small language-model training, quantum-control stress tests, and complete
component ablations. Its central conclusion is deliberately conditional:
full metric structure can remove quadratic conditioning, while scalable
diagonal Hamiltonian–geometric optimization is competitive with AdamW on one
small stochastic workload but is not universally superior. Negative results
showing when geometric, memory, and spectral terms hurt are retained.

The supporting package includes raw per-seed observations, synchronized
time-to-target and peak-memory measurements, bootstrap intervals, paired
parametric and nonparametric tests, Holm correction, an exact dependency lock,
executable theorem checks, and a SHA-256 artifact manifest. Local synthetic
protocols are not represented as official AlgoPerf or DeepOBS results.
