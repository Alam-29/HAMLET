# Journal targeting decision (checked 2026-07-21)

## Locked primary target

**Neural Networks (Elsevier), Article, section “Learning Systems.”** This is
the best match for a paper whose core is a trainable neural-learning optimizer
with mathematical analysis, controlled optimizer comparisons, and explicit
negative results. The journal's official scope includes deep-learning
algorithms and mathematical analyses, and its Article category accepts
original full-length work.

The target is robust to the two common meanings of Q1: the 2024 metrics record
reports **JCR/JIF Q1 and SJR Q1** (JIF 6.3; SJR 1.491). Quartiles remain
year/category dependent, so the corresponding author should still retain the
metric record used by their institution.

## Submission requirements applied

- Single-anonymized review; author names may remain in the manuscript.
- Concise factual abstract, at most 250 words (current abstract: under 250).
- At most six keywords.
- Editable LaTeX source and separately supplied figure files.
- Separate 3--5 item highlights file, at most 85 characters per item.
- Competing-interest statement, funding disclosure, author contributions,
  data/code statement, and generative-AI disclosure.
- The official guide permits full-length Articles and asks authors to choose
  one of five sections; “Learning Systems” is selected here.
- Elsevier's initial-submission workflow accepts editable LaTeX and does not
  impose a strict reference format at this stage. The current consistent
  numbered bibliography can therefore be submitted without a lossy style-only
  rewrite; production styling follows acceptance.

## Fallback

**Applied Soft Computing (Elsevier)** is also JCR Q1 and SJR Q1 in the 2024
record, but its emphasis on integrated soft-computing solutions to real-life
problems creates a higher desk-rejection risk for this mechanism-heavy paper.
It is the fallback only if Neural Networks declines on scope.

## Author-only completion gate

Before upload, the corresponding author must confirm author order,
affiliations, institutional email/postal address, ORCIDs, CRediT roles,
funding, competing interests, originality/concurrent-submission status, and
the public repository DOI. These facts cannot be inferred from code.
